'Round my Indiana homestead wave the cornfields, In the distance lies the woodlands clear and cool. Often times my thoughts revert to scenes of childhood, Where I first received my lessons, natures school. But one thing there is missing in the picture, Without her face it seems so incomplete. I long to see my mother in the door way, As she stood there years ago, her boy to greet. Oh, the moonlight's fair tonight along the Wabash, From the fields there comes the breath of new mown hay, Through the sycamores the candlelight's are gleaming, On the banks of the Wabash faraway
